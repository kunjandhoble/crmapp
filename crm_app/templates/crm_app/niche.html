BUSINESS ANALYST


A business analyst is someone who analyzes business domain and documents organization’s business, processes or systems, assessing the business model or its integration with technology.

Our Expertise and deliverables

Team is very skilled and the typical deliverables are -

  • ➢ Business requirements, i.e. Business plan, Key Performance Indicator, Project plan etc.,
  • ➢ SDLC, Agile and scrum methodology
  • ➢ Functional requirements, i.e. data models, technical specifications, use case scenarios, work instructions, reports.
  • ➢ Non-functional requirements
  • ➢ As-Is processes, i.e. flowcharts
  • ➢ To-Be processes, i.e. flowcharts
  • ➢ Data models, i.e. data requirements expressed as a documented data model of some sort
  • ➢ Business case, a strategic plan containing shareholders’ risk and return
  • ➢ Screen Mockups - Wireframes
  • ➢ Quality Analysis - UAT preparation, Test plan, Test cases, RTM, Defect tracking.
Tools

Microsoft Visio, Excel, ALM, Wireframe tools (Balsamiq, Axure)

DATA ANALYST

A Data Analyst is the professional whose focus of analysis and problem solving relates to data, types of data, and relationships among data elements within a business system or IT system.

Other common titles for this role are: ETL developer, Data Modeler, Business Intelligence Analyst, Data Warehouse Analyst, Systems Analyst, Data Scientist etc.

Our Expertise and deliverables -
  • ➢ Conceptual, Logical and Physical Data Modeling, Relational and Dimensional Modeling for Online Transaction Processing (OLTP) and Online Analytical Processing (OLAP) systems. 
  • ➢ Writing complex SQL queries
  • ➢ Data Werehousing
  • ➢ Data Mapping
  • ➢ ETL (Extract, Transform and Load) of data into a data warehouse/data mart
  • ➢ Basics of Business Intelligence - Data visualization technologies
Tools
  • ● Data Modelling tools –CA ERwin, ER Studio
  • ● ETL tools – Informatica, SSIS, Pentaho, Talend
  • ● Big Data – Hadoop
  • ● Databases - MySQL, SQL Server, Oracle, MS access
  • ● BI tools – Tableau, SSRS
  • ● Excel and VBA macros

FUNCTIONAL ANALYST

  • ➢ The role of the functional analyst (FA) is to capture, consolidate, and communicate the information from the Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) to the rest of the team.
  • ➢ The FA takes the input from the stakeholders and transforms it into something that the development team can understand.
  • ➢ The functional analyst is responsible for ensuring that computer systems remain functional.
  • ➢ Functional analysts act as a liaison between the Information Technology department and company management. Analysts must understand the goals of the employees and adapt the computer systems to these goals. They update the company's information systems in harmony with its overall business objectives.
  • ➢ Functional analysts will look at and review the actual processes within a business. They can then make or suggest a more efficient model and work flow process.
Tools Undertaken -
  • ✓ Kronos – Issue and ticket resolving, timekeeping, Workforce Integration Manager, widgets, and labor level -entries, pay-codes and pay rule or work rules, WIM.
  • ✓ Documentation of functional requirements.
